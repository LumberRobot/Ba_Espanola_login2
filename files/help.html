Why support this advocacy?

When sex education is well-rounded, the advantages go far beyond knowledge of sexually transmitted diseases and other health hazards.To prevent and counteract sexual abuse of children, sexual violence, and sexual exploitation, sexuality education is crucial (Strasbourg, 2020).

Did you know that in some nations, two out of every three girls don't understand what is happening to them when they start menstruating (UNESCO, 2022)?

As they advance from childhood to adulthood, too many young people are given confused and contradictory information about relationships and sex. This has caused young people to want more trustworthy information that will help them live secure, fruitful, and meaningful lives. Children and young people may be more susceptible to risky sexual behaviors and sexual exploitation if they don't receive high-quality, developmentally and age-appropriate sexuality education (UNESCO, 2022).
